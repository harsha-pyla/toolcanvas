How Does the Secure Image to PDF Converter Work?

Unlike third-party conversion portals that force you to upload image documents to their remote backend systems (raising immediate privacy and security concerns), our tool operates with absolute privacy. Your files are processed securely on your device, compiling your images into a single PDF document instantly and safely.

This serverless architecture ensures that your personal documents, IDs, photos, and scanner pages are 100% private. It protects against data leaks, guarantees instant page compilations, and runs completely offline.

The Key Settings for Compiling Image PDFs

Creating a structured PDF from miscellaneous images is simple when using our options:

  • Fit Image (Auto Page Sizing): Each page in the PDF matches the exact aspect ratio and dimensions of its corresponding image. This avoids white borders around horizontal images when compiling them together with vertical documents.
  • Standard Sized Pages (A4 & US Letter): Standardizes every page to international document boundaries. This is ideal if you plan to print the compiled PDF.
  • Auto Orientation: Intelligently rotates individual pages to portrait or landscape depending on whether the source photo is tall or wide.
  • Margining: Controls border spacings. "No Margins" is best for pictures and slides, while "Small" or "Medium" margins create a clean document envelope suited for scans and receipts.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I reorder the pages?

Once your images are uploaded, they appear in a preview list. Use the "Move Up" (▲) and "Move Down" (▼) buttons on each image card to arrange them in the exact order you want them to appear in the PDF document.

Is there a limit on the number of images I can compile?

No. Since the processing runs entirely inside your browser's execution engine, there are no software limits. The capability only depends on your device's memory. For merging very large batches (e.g. 50+ high-res scanner pages), desktop browsers are recommended over mobile devices.

Can I compile transparent PNG or WebP images?

Yes. Transparent pixels are flattened onto a white background to ensure elements like transparent vector logos remain fully visible and readable when rendered in standard PDF viewers.

Can I use this tool offline?

Yes. Once this page is loaded, the entire script library is cached in your browser. You can disconnect from the internet and continue selecting images and compiling PDFs safely offline.
